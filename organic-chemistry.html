Organic Chemistry

Organic chemistry is the branch of chemistry that studies the structure, properties, composition, reactions, and synthesis of carbon-containing compounds. These include hydrocarbons and their derivatives.

  • Hydrocarbons: Compounds composed of only carbon and hydrogen, including alkanes, alkenes, and alkynes.
  • Functional Groups: Specific groups of atoms within molecules that have characteristic properties and chemical behaviors, such as alcohols, amines, carboxylic acids, and ketones.
  • Polymers: Large molecules made up of repeating units called monomers.
