Inorganic Chemistry

Inorganic chemistry is the branch of chemistry that deals with inorganic compounds, which include salts, minerals, metals, and nonmetals. It involves the study of the properties and behavior of inorganic compounds.

  • Coordination Compounds: Complexes consisting of a central metal atom or ion bonded to surrounding ligands.
  • Transition Metals: Elements in the d-block of the periodic table, known for their ability to form multiple oxidation states and colored compounds.
  • Mineralogy: The study of minerals, including their structure, properties, and classification.
