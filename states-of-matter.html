States of Matter

States of matter are the distinct forms that different phases of matter take on. The four fundamental states are solid, liquid, gas, and plasma. Each state has unique properties based on the arrangement and behavior of particles.

  • Solid: Defined shape and volume.
  • Liquid: Defined volume but takes the shape of its container.
  • Gas: Neither defined shape nor volume.
  • Plasma: Ionized gas with free electrons.
