Acids and Bases

Acids and bases are two classes of compounds that have different properties and react with each other to form salts and water. Acids are proton donors (they release H+ ions in solution), while bases are proton acceptors (they release OH- ions in solution).

  • Acids: Sour taste, turn blue litmus paper red, pH less than 7.
  • Bases: Bitter taste, slippery feel, turn red litmus paper blue, pH greater than 7.
  • pH Scale: A measure of the acidity or basicity of a solution, ranging from 0 (most acidic) to 14 (most basic).
