Nuclear Chemistry

Nuclear chemistry is the branch of chemistry that deals with the study of nuclear reactions, radioactive substances, and the changes that occur in the nucleus of atoms.

  • Radioactivity: The spontaneous emission of particles or radiation from the nucleus of an unstable atom.
  • Nuclear Reactions: Reactions that involve changes in the nucleus of an atom, such as fission, fusion, and radioactive decay.
  • Applications: Nuclear chemistry has applications in medicine (radiation therapy), energy production (nuclear power plants), and scientific research.
